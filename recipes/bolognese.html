How to make a proper bolognese sauce

It is the most classic italian dish I could imagine, maybe besides pizza. It embodies the meatiness and tomatoes that in such great strength scream: italian.

All it takes is a couple ingredients to enhance your basic tomato sauce to a proper meat sauce that the dish is supposed to be.

The ingredients are: carrot, celery, parmesan cheese, beef stock and red wine. The part that stays the same are tomatoes and minced meat. Let's get down to business.

Ingredients

  • 300g minced beef
  • 300g minced pork
  • 200g bacon(or guanciale if you have such delicacy on you)
  • 3 medium carrots
  • 3 celery sticks
  • 500ml of beef stock
  • 150ml red wine
  • 50g parmesan cheese
  • 3 medium white onions
  • 50g tomato paste
  • 300ml tomato passata
  • a couple basil leaves
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a couple cloves of garlic

  1. Prepare your vegetables: white onions in small cubes as well as carrots and celery
  2. Slice your bacon (or guanciale) into small cubes
  3. Pour oil into your pot - yes pot, you will need the capacity of a pot to fit it all (burner on medium-high heat), heat it up until it is warm and not smoking and behaves like water
  4. Toss in your vegetables, starting with onions and garlic until fragrant
  5. Remove your veggies once they are soft into a bowl
  6. It's time for bacon: set your burner to medium and make sure all the fat gets rendered and not burnt
  7. Once your bacon is browned, it's time for minced meat to come into the party: put both of beef and pork into the pot
  8. Stir everything often until meat starts to become grey/brown
  9. Toss in the veggies
  10. Put the tomato paste and passata into the pan
  11. Pour in the beef stock
  12. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cover with a lid
  13. Put the parmesan into the pot as a whole piece
  14. Stir occasionally until reduced and the meat sauce is thick
  15. Voila! You can prepare pasta for a proper pasta bolognese, or just eat straight out of the pot with a fork! (joking)
