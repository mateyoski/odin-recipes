How to make a proper carbonara

Carbonara is such a glorious meal that will pleasure your guests' and loved ones' palate. It is so simple to make yet the combination of fatty pancetta and sharp pecorino cheese will make any mouth water with how complex the taste is. Considered an abomination by Italians when made with cream, proper carbonara has nothing of this sort. It simply uses a couple of techniques all pasta enjoyers and makers should know.

Ingredients

  • 250g spaghetti or tagliatelle
  • 100g bacon or pancetta
  • 50g pecorino cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • a handful of parsley
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves of garlic

  1. Boil a pot of water, make sure to salt it generously
  2. Into a bowl put the whites and yolks of 2 eggs
  3. Add salt pepper and grated pecorino into the bowl
  4. Mix until homogenised
  5. When the water is boiling, put your pasta into the pot
  6. In a pan over medium heat render the fat of the bacon/pancetta until the meat browns
  7. When the pasta becomes al dente, reserve 100ml of water before draining the pasta
  8. Remove the pan from the stove and put the drained pasta into the pan
  9. Mix the pasta water into the egg and cheese mixture
  10. Mix it with the fat and bacon/pancetta
  11. Pour the mixture slowly into the pan, stirring the pan contents vigorously
  12. Keep stirring until homogenised
  13. Serve it with grated cheese on top and extra bacon/pancetta from the pan
